What is DSP?

In the real world... there are analog signals, that are in continuous time domain. Modern computers cannot process continuous data -- atleast not yet, so we convert the signal from continuous-time domain into discrete-time domain -- and our signal now becomes a digital signal. This is achieved using an Analog-to-Digital Converter (which I will discuss later in the blog).

Digital Signal Processing is an umbrella term used for mathematical algorithms and tools --- that computers use --- to analyze and process real world signals. So in layman terms, this is pure math! A perfect place to begin my journey to " mathematical maturity ".
