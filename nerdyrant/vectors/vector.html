I came across a question that shook my foundational concepts in vectors. This involved the use of cencepts like linear independence and collinearity. Check out this question below:

Question: Prove that the vectors \(\,\,\, \vec{a} = 3i + j - 2k, \vec{b} = -i + 3j + 4k, \vec{c} = 4i - 2j - 6k \,\,\, \) can form the sides of a triangle. Find lengths of the medians of the triangle.

This is an example question from the book Higher Engineering Mathematics by B.V. Ramana. The question asks us to check if the given vectors can form the sides of a triangle or not. A vector can be used to define points in a coordinate system, but they can also just be vectors --- lines with a direction. In this case, they were just lines, and so the question meant --- use the vectors to form a triangle.

My first intuition was to prove somehow, that the sum of adjacent sides of a triangle must be greater than the other side.
