Documenting Life


May 11, 2025

For as long as I can remember, life has always been easy going for me. I didn't have to worry about doing something meaningful up until I graduated 10th grade from ICSE. COVID-19 just started back then, it was early March, 2020. I had to choose between staying back at my former school, or change schools, since I couldn't go back there anyways. Quarantine and lockdown were setting all over the country, and the globe. So I did what most of my other colleagues did -- change schools.

I got enrolled to a local school in my hometown for the next two years; +2. But the next two years were nothing like I could have expected. I knew I needed a purpose. 11th & 12th grade pretty much decide what you're gonna do for the most of your academic life. And I was aware of that, but all I lacked was clarity. For me, an average student, opting for science, JEE seemed like the next big thing (along with 11 Lakh other aspirants across India). And so I began prepping for JEE like anyone else would. And if you've ever prepared for a competitive exam of that magnitude, you'd know right away the bullshit I was stepping into. JEE isn't your regular "strawberry-cupcake class test", it is .... tedious.

And that is precisely when I lost it. I lost clarity the moment I'd decided to prep for JEE. I'm not downplaying my shortcommings or whining about my lack of competence, I'm just trying to put a finger on what happened where. And if you're reading this, you should know, not everyone gets to taste the nectar of success. Pretty cheesy line (ik)... But it is what it is. I've longed for clarity for as long as I can remember. I never really knew what I wanted to do in life. And a part of me is glad about it, I have this feeling that maybe, just maybe ... unexpected things will come my way. But thats just delusion speaking at this point. Without a goal, I'm as good as dead.

It’s wild how silence can be so loud. Those early days of the pandemic — with the streets empty and my inbox full of motivational quotes — felt less like a pause and more like a void. I wasn’t choosing a school. I was choosing what kind of adult I might become. And even that felt too big, too soon. In hindsight, I don’t think I was choosing between schools. I was choosing between the comfort of what I knew, and the discomfort of becoming someone new. And the thing about discomfort is… it doesn't ask for permission to change you. It just does. I think, deep down, I was hoping the world would freeze just long enough for me to catch up. But time, even in lockdown, never really stops — it just changes shape. And while everything around me stood still, I could feel this pressure building, like I had to emerge from it all with some kind of 'purpose' or 'plan' or 'newfound clarity'. Spoiler alert: I didn’t. I just floated through it, like most of us did, pretending we were fine while quietly falling apart in invisible ways.
